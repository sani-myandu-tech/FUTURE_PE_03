H1: Eating in Durban on a Student Budget: How to Eat Well Without
Spending Everything You Have

Student life in Durban is many things — exciting, busy, and often broke.
Between rent, data, and textbooks, food is usually the first budget to
take a hit. But eating badly shouldn't be the price of studying.


H2: The Student Food Problem in Durban

Most budget food options in Durban fall into one of two categories: cheap
but terrible, or decent but too expensive. Students end up cycling through
the same two or three options until even those lose their appeal.

The result? Bad eating habits, low energy, and the dreaded 3pm brain fog
right when you need to focus most.

H2: Practical Tips for Eating Well on a Budget in Durban

H3: 1. Eat at off-peak times
Many cafés in Durban offer smaller portions or deals during quieter hours.
Lunch between 11am and 12pm or after 2pm tends to be faster and cheaper.

H3: 2. Look for combo meals and daily specials
Places like Urban Bites Café offer daily specials that give you more food
for less money. A main plus a drink for under R100 is entirely achievable
if you know where to look.

H3: 3. Prioritise speed and proximity
Between lectures, time matters. Choose spots close to campus that can get
you in and out in under 20 minutes without sacrificing quality.

H3: 4. Avoid the overpriced meal-delivery trap
Delivery apps add service fees, delivery fees, and surge pricing. Walking
to a local café is almost always cheaper and faster in Durban's city areas.
